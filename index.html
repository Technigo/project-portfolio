Summary section

This is the section where you summarise what you have been doing in the past and what you are wanting to do in the future. This gives the reader an idea of who you are. Keep it short and personal.

Tech section

Give a short summary/name dropping of all the technologies, tools, libraries and things that you know and will know during this course. It's really the more the merrier here.

Feature section

Put the projects that you are most proud of here. Make sure to mention all technologies, libraries or APIs that you have used. Don't feel like you are repeating yourself. As a recruiter looking for a React developer – the more React is mentioned – the better. If you have other code projects that are not related to this course, feel free to add them too. Link your projects to the Github repo and **make sure the whole area is a link**, meaning the image, headline and text. Also add a link to where the visitors can see the project live. (If you rather do the other way around, making the image and headline link to the deployed version of your project and then have a link to where they can see the code, that is also okay.)

Other project

Put all of your other projects here. The more the merrier.

My thoughts section

This is the area where you really can present yourself and your thoughts about code. It's extremely helpful for recruiters to pick your brain a little and see that you don't just code – you also reflect. During the career coaching program, you will be asked to post reflections to LinkedIn connected to the different topics covered in the boot camp, this is an excellent opportunity to showcase your reflections to future employers. You can leave this empty for now and get back to it when you have come further along in the career coaching program. When you do add links in this section in the future **make sure that the whole area is clickable and a link**, meaning the image, headline and text.

Skills
