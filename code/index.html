Publication #1

Ever wonder why the top and bottom of your hand have very different shapes? This different anatomy and functionality is the result of a gene called Lmx1b. Studying the genetic pathways that control how limbs are formed has helped us understand many basic genetic pathways of embryonic development.

Publication #2

Another facinating question why do we have 5 fingers? Why not 4? Why not 6? The interaction of Sonic Hedgehog (SHH) and Fibroblast Growth Factor (FGF) is a well known genetic loop that controls this process, and changes in this loop will lead to an abnormal number of fingers. Here we look at how polydactly, meaning hands with more than five fingers, can occur using a mouse model which has an overexpression of SHH leading to polydactly.

Publication #3

EMT (epithelial mesenchymal transitoin) is when cells change state.This is a tool used by devloping tumors to leave the main tumor and travel through the bloodstream looking for secondary sites to metastisize. Using a possible new type of chemothereapy agent we tested if blocking ribosome biogenesis could in fact stop tumors from creating metastisis in a mouse model of breast cancer. This inhibitor shows great promise for future breast cancer treatments.
