Detection of genes regulated by Lmx1b during limb dorsalization

Ever wonder why the top and bottom of your hand have very different shapes? This different anatomy and functionality comes from a gene called Lmx1b. Studying the genetic pathways that control how limbs are formed has helped us understand many basic pathways for organ development. This study is one of many that hopes to contribute to the basic science that will lead to a bright new future of gene therapy in the womb.

ESCRT-II/Vps25 constrains digit number by endosome-mediated selective modulation of FGF-SHH signaling

Another facinating question why do we have 5 fingers? Why not 4? Why not 6? The interaction of Sonic Hedgehog (SHH) and Fibroblast Growth Factor (FGF) is a well known genetic loop that controls this process, and changes in this loop will lead to an abnormal number of fingers. Here we look at how polydactly meaning hands with more than five fingers can occur using a mouse model which has polydactly.

Ribosome biogenesis during cell cycle arrest fuels EMT in development and disease

EMT (epithelial mesenchymal transitoin) is when cells change state.This is a tool used by devloping tumors to leave the main tumor and travel through the bloodstream looking for secondary sites to metastisize. Using a possible new type of chemothereapy agent we tested if blocking ribosome biogenesis could in fact stop tumors from creating metastisis in a mouse model of breast cancer. This inhibitor shows great promise for future breast cancer treatments and should be tested further.
